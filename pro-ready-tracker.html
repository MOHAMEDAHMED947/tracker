Non-Negotiables

Every Day

  • Sleep 8–9 hours. This is where speed and muscle are actually built. Non-negotiable at 19.
  • Protein ~1.8 g per kg bodyweight, spread over 3–4 meals. Carbs before and after field sessions.
  • Hydrate: 2.5–3.5 L/day; add electrolytes on double-session or hot days.
  • Off days: 20–30 min walk + 10 min juggling or wall touches + 8 min foam rolling (quads, adductors, calves, glutes). Light movement beats total rest.

Groin Rules

  • The Copenhagen adductor exercise appears twice a week — it cuts groin injury risk by roughly 40% in footballers. Never skip it.
  • Mild muscle work-soreness after Copenhagens is normal for the first 2 weeks. Sharp or pinching groin pain during sprinting or cutting is not — stop the session, ice, and drop back one Copenhagen level.
  • Always complete the full warm-up before any sprint or change-of-direction work. Cold adductors are how re-injuries happen.

Fascia Principles

  • Fascia adapts slowly (months, not days) — small, frequent, springy doses beat rare heavy ones. That's why elastic work appears in every Day 4.
  • Think quiet, bouncy, effortless: land silently, spend minimal time on the ground, let the tendons do the work.
  • Vary angles and directions — fascia responds to novelty. Never do bounds in only one line.
  • Foam rolling = hydration and glide for the tissue. Slow rolls, 30–60 s per area, breathe.
Read this before you start: This program is a training plan, not medical advice. You've had a groin issue — if you feel sharp pain (not normal muscle burn) at any point, stop and see a physio before continuing. Progress the weights only when technique is perfect. If you miss a day, just do the next session — don't stack two hard days back-to-back.
